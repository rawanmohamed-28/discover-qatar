Explore Qatar’s Essence

Discover what makes Qatar unique — from its climate and landscape to its modern cities.

Climate

Qatar enjoys long sunny days, mild winters, and warm evenings that highlight the desert’s natural beauty.

Geography

Located on a peninsula in the Arabian Gulf, Qatar is known for its desert dunes, coastal plains, and sparkling shoreline.

Towns & Cities

From the bustling capital Doha to Al Khor and Al Wakrah, each city reflects Qatar’s blend of tradition and innovation.

Traditions of Qatar

Experience Qatar’s authentic heritage — from its hospitality to its timeless cultural symbols.

Majlis

Meaning of the word: Majlis is an Arabic term meaning ‘a place of sitting’.

Traditionally male-only spaces: Women have private majlis inside the home.

Dates & Arabic coffee: Guests in the majlis are served dates and coffee from a dallah pot.

Mosques

Qatar is home to stunning mosques that reflect Islamic architecture, such as the State Grand Mosque, Education City Mosque, and Katara Mosque — must-visits for tourists.

Arabic Coffee

Arabic coffee (gahwa) is a symbol of Qatari hospitality, traditionally served in small cups alongside dates, reflecting warmth and generosity.

Oud

Luxury: First-grade agarwood can cost as much as $100,000 per kilogram, making it a symbol of prestige.

Ancient Roots: Oud has been used since 1400 B.C.E for spiritual, medicinal, and aromatic purposes.

Rarity: Only about 2% of agarwood trees naturally produce oud resin, adding to its value.

Bukhoor

What is Bukhoor made from? It’s Middle Eastern incense made of wood chips soaked in perfumed oil, resin, and herbs — burned for fragrance during gatherings.

Pearls

Before oil, Qatar was world-famous for its natural pearls — once the backbone of its economy and a symbol of purity and elegance.
